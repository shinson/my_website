Born and raised in Atlanta, I moved to Chicago to attend the University of Chicago. After many grueling years of selling my soul to academic gods (Plato, Smith, etc.), I decided to have some fun. I learned Arabic and studied abroad in Jerusalem and Irbid, Jordan. After a short stint in Middle East research, I was not feeling the impact of the work I was doing, so thankfully with a little bit of luck and a good sales pitch, I landed a job in Syrian humanitarian relief.

I have always incorporated learning tech into my daily life, but it wasn't until January 2014 did I take this passion into full speed. With the help of the tremendous DC women in tech movement I have learned way more skills than i ever did in college. With a focus in web development and programming, I hope to use these skills to bridge the gap between technology and humanitarian/development programs.
